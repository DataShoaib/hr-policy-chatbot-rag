# HR Policy Document: Code of Conduct & Disciplinary Policy
## Document ID: HRP-003 | Version: 1.5 | Effective: July 1, 2024

---

## 1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes the standards of professional behavior expected from all TechCorp India Pvt. Ltd. employees and defines the consequences for violations. It ensures a respectful, safe, and productive work environment.

---

## 2. CODE OF CONDUCT

### 2.1 Professional Behavior
- Treat all colleagues, clients, and stakeholders with respect and dignity
- Maintain confidentiality of business, financial, and personnel information
- Avoid conflicts of interest; declare them immediately when they arise
- Report to work on time; attendance is mandatory per agreed schedule
- Use company assets (laptop, software, data) only for authorized business purposes
- Represent the company positively at external events, social media, and public forums

### 2.2 Workplace Harassment Policy (POSH Compliance)
- **Zero tolerance** for sexual harassment (as per POSH Act 2013)
- **Internal Complaints Committee (ICC):** Present at all offices
- **ICC Chairperson:** Priya Mehta (priya.mehta.icc@techcorp.in)
- **Complaint Procedure:**
  1. Submit written complaint to ICC within 90 days of incident
  2. ICC conducts inquiry within 90 days of receiving complaint
  3. Findings reported to management within 10 days of inquiry completion
- **Confidentiality:** Identity of complainant protected at all times
- **Retaliation:** Strictly prohibited; retaliation is itself a terminable offense

### 2.3 Anti-Discrimination Policy
- No discrimination based on: caste, religion, gender, age, disability, sexual orientation, marital status, pregnancy, or national origin
- Equal opportunity employer in hiring, promotion, and all employment decisions

### 2.4 Data Privacy & Confidentiality
- **MNDA (Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement):** Signed at joining
- **Customer data:** Must not be accessed without business need; violations trigger immediate suspension
- **GDPR and IT Act compliance:** Mandatory for all employees handling customer data
- **Data breach reporting:** Must be reported to security@techcorp.in within 1 hour of discovery

### 2.5 Social Media Policy
- Do NOT post confidential business information, unreleased product info, financial data, or client names
- Do NOT make disparaging remarks about the company, competitors, or colleagues
- Personal opinions shared publicly must be clearly stated as personal views
- Brand mentions must comply with Marketing team guidelines

### 2.6 Dress Code
- **Office days:** Smart casual (Monday to Thursday), Casual Friday
- **Client visits / presentations:** Business formals required
- **Offensive attire:** Prohibited at all times (clothing with offensive language, symbols)

---

## 3. CONFLICT OF INTEREST

### 3.1 What Constitutes a Conflict
- Working for a competitor (part-time or freelance)
- Vendor relationship where employee or family member has financial interest
- Hiring a family member without disclosure and HR approval
- Personal investment in competing companies beyond ₹1 lakh

### 3.2 Disclosure Process
- Submit **COI Declaration Form** on HRMS portal annually (due: April 15th)
- Immediate disclosure required when a new conflict arises mid-year
- HR and Legal will determine if conflict is material and how to manage it

## 4. DISCIPLINARY PROCESS

### 4.1 Types of Misconduct

**Minor Misconduct (Informal Warning Level):**
- Occasional tardiness or unexcused absences
- Minor policy non-compliance (e.g., not wearing ID card)
- Unprofessional communication
- Minor expense policy violations

**Major Misconduct (Formal Action Required):**
- Repeated minor misconduct after warning
- Insubordination or disrespect to management
- Serious violation of data policies
- Harassment (non-sexual)
- Substance use at workplace
- Unauthorized disclosure of business information

**Gross Misconduct (Immediate Termination Eligible):**
- Theft, fraud, or financial irregularity
- Sexual harassment
- Violence or threats in workplace
- Sharing confidential data with competitors
- Willful damage to company property
- Serious violation of POSH Act
- Forging documents

---

## 5. DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE

### 5.1 Progressive Discipline Steps

**Step 1: Verbal Warning**
- Manager issues verbal warning for minor misconduct
- Documented in HRMS as informal note
- Employee given 30 days to correct behavior

**Step 2: Written Warning (Show Cause Notice)**
- Issued if behavior continues after verbal warning OR for first-time major misconduct
- Employee must respond within 7 working days
- HR must be involved from this step
- Remains on record for 12 months

**Step 3: Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)**
- Duration: 30 to 90 days
- Specific, measurable targets set
- Weekly check-ins with manager and HR
- Failure to meet PIP targets leads to termination

**Step 4: Suspension (with or without pay)**
- Used during investigation of serious misconduct
- Maximum suspension period: 90 days
- Pay during suspension: Full pay (pending investigation result)

**Step 5: Termination**
- With notice (as per employment contract)
- Without notice for Gross Misconduct (immediate termination)

### 5.2 Employee Rights During Disciplinary Process
- Right to be informed of charges in writing
- Right to respond and present evidence
- Right to bring a colleague (not a lawyer) to disciplinary hearing
- Right to appeal decision within 15 days to Senior HR Director

## 6. GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL

### 6.1 Grievance Process
1. **Level 1:** Discuss with immediate manager (3 working days)
2. **Level 2:** Raise with HR Business Partner if unresolved (7 working days)
3. **Level 3:** Escalate to HR Director (14 working days)
4. **Level 4:** Ombudsman / External Mediator (for unresolved cases)

### 6.2 Anonymous Reporting
- **Ethics Hotline:** 1800-ETHICS-TC (24x7)
- **Email:** ethics@techcorp.in (reviewed by independent committee)
- No retaliation policy protects whistleblowers

---

## 7. EXIT AND SEPARATION CONDUCT

- Employees must return all company assets (laptop, access cards, documents) on last working day
- Non-disclosure obligations continue post-employment (2 years)
- Non-solicitation clause: Cannot solicit TechCorp clients or employees for 1 year post exit
- Non-compete clause: Cannot join direct competitors for 6 months (for L4 and above)
