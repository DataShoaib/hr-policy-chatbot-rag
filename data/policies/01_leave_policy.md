# HR Policy Document: Leave Management Policy
## Document ID: HRP-001 | Version: 2.1 | Effective: January 1, 2024

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Leave Management Policy outlines the types of leaves available to all full-time, part-time, and contractual employees of TechCorp India Pvt. Ltd. This policy applies to all employees across all departments, levels, and locations including Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi NCR, Hyderabad, and Pune offices.

---

## 2. TYPES OF LEAVES

### 2.1 Casual Leave (CL)
- **Entitlement:** 12 days per calendar year
- **Accrual:** 1 day per month, credited on the 1st of each month
- **Maximum consecutive days:** 3 days without manager approval; more than 3 days requires HR approval
- **Carry forward:** NOT allowed. Unused CL lapses on December 31st
- **Encashment:** NOT applicable for Casual Leave
- **Application notice:** Minimum 1 day prior notice required (except emergencies)
- **Eligible from:** Day 1 of joining

### 2.2 Sick Leave (SL)
- **Entitlement:** 10 days per calendar year
- **Accrual:** Credited fully on April 1st (start of financial year)
- **Medical certificate:** Required for more than 2 consecutive sick days
- **Carry forward:** Up to 5 unused days can be carried forward to next year (maximum accumulation: 30 days)
- **Encashment:** Allowed at retirement or resignation (50% of accumulated balance)
- **Application notice:** Inform manager on the same day by 10:00 AM

### 2.3 Earned Leave / Privilege Leave (PL/EL)
- **Entitlement:** 18 days per calendar year
- **Accrual:** 1.5 days per month after completing 6 months of service
- **Minimum application:** 3 days minimum per application
- **Maximum consecutive days:** 30 days at a stretch (with approval)
- **Carry forward:** Up to 30 days per year (maximum accumulation: 90 days)
- **Encashment:** Allowed during service (maximum 15 days per year) and at full & final settlement
- **Application notice:** Minimum 7 days advance notice required
- **Sandwich rule:** Weekends and public holidays between EL are counted as leave days

### 2.4 Maternity Leave (ML)
- **Entitlement:** 26 weeks (182 days) for first two children; 12 weeks for third child onwards
- **Pre-delivery:** Up to 8 weeks before expected delivery date
- **Post-delivery:** Remaining period after childbirth
- **Miscarriage/Medical termination:** 6 weeks leave
- **Adoption:** 12 weeks for women adopting a child below 3 months of age
- **Commissioning mothers:** 12 weeks from date of birth of child
- **Eligibility:** Employee must have worked for minimum 80 days in the 12 months preceding delivery
- **Pay:** Full pay during maternity leave (100% of last drawn salary)
- **Creche facility:** Employees returning from maternity leave are entitled to 4 visits to creche per day

### 2.5 Paternity Leave
- **Entitlement:** 15 days (paid)
- **Eligibility:** Male employees (biological/adoptive fathers)
- **Timing:** Within 30 days of childbirth/adoption
- **Application:** 7 days prior notice or within 2 days in case of emergency delivery

### 2.6 Bereavement Leave
- **Immediate family (Spouse, Children, Parents, Siblings):** 5 days paid leave
- **Extended family (In-laws, Grandparents):** 3 days paid leave
- **Other relatives:** 1 day paid leave
- **Application:** Inform HR/manager as soon as possible; documentation may be required

### 2.7 Marriage Leave
- **Entitlement:** 5 days (one-time benefit during service)
- **Notice:** 15 days advance notice required
- **Documentation:** Marriage certificate to be submitted within 30 days

### 2.8 Public Holidays
- **National Holidays (Mandatory):** Republic Day (Jan 26), Independence Day (Aug 15), Gandhi Jayanti (Oct 2)
- **Festival Holidays:** 7 optional holidays from a declared list of 14 festivals
- **Employees must inform HR of chosen optional holidays by January 31st each year**

### 2.9 Compensatory Off (Comp-Off)
- **Eligibility:** Employees who work on weekends or public holidays at management request
- **Accrual:** 1 day Comp-Off for each day worked
- **Validity:** Must be utilized within 60 days of earning
- **Maximum accumulation:** 5 days at any given time

### 2.10 Work From Home (WFH) Leave
- **Entitlement:** Up to 3 days per week for eligible roles (as per WFH eligibility matrix)
- **Not a substitute for Leave:** WFH cannot replace sick leave or casual leave
- **Manager approval:** Required for WFH; blanket approvals not permitted

## 3. LEAVE APPLICATION PROCESS

### 3.1 Applying for Leave
1. Log in to **HR Portal** (hrms.techcorp.in)
2. Navigate to **Leave Management > Apply Leave**
3. Select leave type, dates, and reason
4. Submit for manager approval
5. Approved leaves auto-deducted from balance

### 3.2 Emergency Leave
- Call/WhatsApp manager immediately
- Update on HRMS portal on the same day or next working day
- Supporting documents to be submitted within 3 days of returning

### 3.3 Leave Without Pay (LWP)
- Applicable when all leave balance is exhausted
- Requires explicit approval from Department Head and HR
- More than 10 LWP days in a year triggers performance review
- LWP affects annual increment calculation (pro-rated)

---

## 4. LEAVE ENCASHMENT RULES

| Leave Type | During Service | At Separation |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| Casual Leave | Not allowed | Not allowed |
| Sick Leave | Not allowed | 50% of balance |
| Earned Leave | Max 15 days/year | 100% of balance (max 90 days) |
| Comp-Off | Not allowed | Forfeited |

**Tax treatment:** Leave encashment during service is fully taxable. At retirement, exemption up to ₹25 lakhs under Section 10(10AA) of Income Tax Act.

---

## 5. SPECIAL PROVISIONS

### 5.1 Probation Period
- New employees on probation (first 6 months) are eligible only for CL (6 days pro-rated) and SL (5 days pro-rated)
- EL accrual begins after probation completion

### 5.2 Part-time Employees
- Leave entitlements are pro-rated based on working hours

### 5.3 Long Service Leave
- Employees completing 5 years: Additional 5 days EL (one-time)
- Employees completing 10 years: Additional 10 days EL (one-time)

---

## 6. POLICY VIOLATIONS

- Taking leave without approval = Salary deduction + disciplinary action
- Misrepresentation of reasons = Termination of employment
- Frequent uninformed absences = Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
